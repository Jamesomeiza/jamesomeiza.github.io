What I'm Doing Now

Inspired by nownownow.com — a snapshot of what I'm focused on at this moment in my life and career.

Last updated: January 2025 — Abuja, Nigeria

Working On
Bonga 3D / Bonga North 4D — Offshore Nigeria

Currently at sea as Infield Processor on Shell Nigeria's combined 3D and 4D seismic acquisition campaign over OML 118. This is Nigeria's flagship deepwater field — the country's first deep-water production. Real-time QC and infield processing of streamer data across full survey geometry.

Ongoing since August 2024
OML 40 Land Acquisition Geometry Design

Between offshore rotations, contributing to acquisition design for the OML 40 3D Land Seismic in the Niger Delta — optimising fold, azimuth, and offset distributions for PSTM imaging in a structurally complex onshore environment.

NNPC Energy Services, 2024
Studying & Learning
Seismic Inversion Theory & Practice

Self-directed study in simultaneous pre-stack seismic inversion and AVO analysis. Working through the mathematical foundations of Zoeppritz equations, linearised approximations (Shuey, Aki-Richards), and their connection to rock physics diagnostics. Goal: MSc-level fluency before graduate school applications.

Python for Geophysics

Building practical skills in scientific Python: numpy, scipy, matplotlib, and the bruges geophysics library. Working on scripts for Vp/Vs crossplot analysis and simple 1D inversion exercises on synthetic data. Transitioning from MATLAB.

MSc Geophysics Application Research

Researching graduate programmes aligned with my interests in seismic inversion and quantitative interpretation. Reviewing faculty profiles, thesis databases, and scholarship opportunities at institutions in the UK, Australia, and Canada.

Thinking About
What 4D seismic will look like in 10 years

Permanent reservoir monitoring (PRM) arrays, distributed acoustic sensing (DAS), and continuous seismic monitoring are reshaping what "time-lapse" means. I'm thinking about the processing and inversion challenges this creates — and whether there's a research gap I could work on.

The geophysics of the energy transition in Nigeria

Nigeria's geological basement hosts geothermal potential that is almost entirely unstudied. The same techniques used for hydrocarbon exploration — seismic reflection, MT, gravity — could map geothermal resources. Is there a realistic pathway for this as part of Nigeria's energy diversification strategy?

How to write a genuinely good research statement

Reading examples from successful applicants to Edinburgh, Imperial, and Curtin. The best ones don't just say what the person wants to study — they explain why that specific problem matters now, and why this person is the one to work on it.

Outreach & Community
NAPE Young Professionals — Geophysics Mentorship

Mentoring undergraduate geoscience students through the Nigerian Association of Petroleum Explorationists Young Professionals network. Focusing on career transitions from university into industry — especially demystifying seismic data acquisition and the skills that actually get you hired.

Writing Geophysics Explainers for This Blog

Drafting plain-language articles on seismic inversion, 4D monitoring, and Nigeria's frontier basin geology. The goal is to make exploration geophysics accessible to curious students who've never encountered it — and to force myself to articulate concepts precisely.
