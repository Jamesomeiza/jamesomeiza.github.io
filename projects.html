Primer

What is Marine Seismic Acquisition?

Marine seismic acquisition is the process of generating, recording, and processing acoustic energy waves in the ocean to produce detailed images of the rock layers beneath the sea floor. A survey vessel tows an array of air guns that release controlled bursts of compressed air, generating pressure waves that travel downward, reflect off subsurface rock interfaces, and are recorded by hydrophones towed in cables (streamers) behind the vessel.

In a 3D survey, a dense grid of shot points and receivers is used to produce a full three-dimensional volume of the subsurface — allowing geologists and geophysicists to see structures, faults, and potential reservoir bodies in detail.

A 4D (time-lapse) survey repeats a previously acquired 3D survey over the same area after a period of production, enabling operators to detect changes in fluid saturation and pressure within the reservoir — a critical tool for managing producing fields and optimising recovery.

Bonga Field 3D Seismic Survey

OML 118 · Niger Delta Deepwater

3D Acquisition

The Bonga field, operated by Shell Nigeria Exploration and Production Company (SNEPCo), was Nigeria's first deep-water oil and gas development. Located in OML 118 approximately 120 kilometres offshore in water depths of around 1000 metres, Bonga has been producing since 2005 and remains one of the country's most significant offshore assets.

The Bonga 3D seismic survey was designed to provide a high-resolution, full-fold subsurface image of the field and its surrounding area. The survey employed a modern ocean-bottom cable (OBC) and towed-streamer configuration to achieve dense spatial sampling and broad azimuthal coverage.

As Infield Processor, James was responsible for real-time QC of incoming shot gathers, near-field noise attenuation, navigation data QC and merging, and daily reporting on data quality metrics. His work ensured that the seismic data met the project's specifications and client requirements, and that any acquisition problems were identified and corrected in the field.

Bonga North Field 4D Survey

OML 118 · Niger Delta Deepwater

4D Monitoring

The Bonga North 4D survey was a time-lapse seismic monitoring campaign over the northern extension of the Bonga field. The 4D programme was designed to image changes in reservoir fluid contacts and saturation following years of production, providing Shell with the information needed to optimise the remaining field life.

The repeatability of 4D seismic acquisition is critical — subtle differences between the baseline and monitor surveys must be minimised to isolate the true production signal from noise. James's infield processing role was particularly important in ensuring rigorous QC of navigation, geometry, and data quality parameters against the baseline survey specifications.

BGP / NNPC JV · Chevron

Agbami Field 4D Survey

OML 127 · Niger Delta Deepwater

4D Monitoring

The Agbami field, operated by Chevron Nigeria, is one of the largest deepwater oil accumulations ever discovered in sub-Saharan Africa, with estimated recoverable reserves in excess of one billion barrels of oil. Located in OML 127 at water depths of over 1400 metres, Agbami has been a flagship deepwater development since first production in 2008.

The 4D survey was conducted to monitor reservoir depletion and assist Chevron's reservoir engineers in identifying by-passed pay zones and optimising infill well placement. The ultra-deepwater environment and the scale of the reservoir made this one of the most technically challenging projects in the campaign.

James contributed as Infield Processor, overseeing QC workflows, data pre-processing, and coordination with the onshore processing team to ensure seamless handover of high-quality data.

Egina Field 4D Survey

OML 130 · Niger Delta Deepwater

4D Monitoring

The Egina field is operated by TotalEnergies EP Nigeria in OML 130, at water depths of approximately 1500 metres. Production from Egina began in 2018, making it one of the newest — and largest — deepwater developments in Nigeria, with production capacity of around 200,000 barrels per day.

The 4D monitoring survey was part of TotalEnergies' reservoir surveillance strategy for the early field life of Egina, providing critical data to validate reservoir models and understand depletion patterns in the subsalt turbidite reservoirs. James served as Infield Processor, maintaining data quality standards and producing daily QC reports for the client representative.

Preowei Field 3D Survey

OML 130 · Niger Delta Deepwater

3D Acquisition

The Preowei 3D survey was conducted over the Preowei discovery in OML 130, also operated by TotalEnergies. Preowei is a field-development candidate adjacent to the producing Egina complex, and the 3D survey was designed to provide improved subsurface imaging for reservoir definition and well planning.

James's contribution as Infield Processor included managing the QCQC workflow for source and receiver positioning, monitoring seismic data quality in real time, and ensuring compliance with the project's fold, offset, and azimuth requirements.
