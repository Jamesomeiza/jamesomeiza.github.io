Outreach & Mentorship

Building the next generation of Nigerian geoscientists through mentorship, workshops, and professional development.

Philosophy

Why Outreach Matters

Nigeria trains thousands of geoscience graduates every year, yet many leave university without a clear picture of the technical depth demanded by the industry, or the academic pathways that could lead to research careers. James believes that bridging this gap is both a personal responsibility and a professional investment in the future of Nigerian exploration.

His outreach philosophy is straightforward: show students what is possible, give them the vocabulary and conceptual tools to navigate the geoscience world, and connect them with the professional networks that will support their careers.

He participates in the Nigerian Association of Petroleum Explorationists (NAPE) Young Professionals program, which organises mentorship pairing, campus workshops, and professional development events for Nigerian geoscience students.

🌊

NAPE Young Professionals

The Nigerian Association of Petroleum Explorationists Young Professionals program connects early-career geoscientists and students with experienced industry practitioners for mentorship, guidance, and professional networking.

Activities

Outreach Activities

🎓
University Workshops

James participates in workshops at Nigerian universities, presenting on careers in marine seismic exploration, the science of 3D and 4D seismic, and pathways from a geophysics degree to the offshore industry. These workshops are designed to make technical careers feel accessible and achievable for undergraduates.

🤝
One-on-One Mentorship

Through the NAPE Young Professionals program, James provides individual mentorship to students and recent graduates navigating the early stages of their geoscience careers. Topics covered include technical skills development, graduate school application strategy, CV and interview preparation, and industry awareness.

🏆
Basin Evaluation Competitions

James supports and participates in student basin evaluation competitions — events where teams of geoscience students apply regional geological and geophysical analysis to evaluate a real sedimentary basin for hydrocarbon prospectivity. These competitions are excellent preparation for industry workflows and technical presentations.

✍️
Technical Writing & Blog

James publishes accessible articles on geophysics, seismic methods, and the Nigerian energy landscape on this website. The aim is to provide quality technical content in plain language — making the science of exploration geophysics available to a broader audience, including students who may not yet have access to journal articles.

💬
Career Q&A Sessions

James participates in Q&A sessions organised by university departments and professional societies, answering questions about the reality of working offshore, the skills that matter most in marine seismic operations, and how students can best position themselves for entry-level roles in the geoscience industry.

🔬
Research Guidance

For undergraduate students working on final-year geophysics projects, James offers informal guidance on methodology, data interpretation, and scientific writing. Having recently completed his own undergraduate research in potential field methods, he is familiar with the challenges facing students attempting original geophysical work with limited data and resources.
