Research Vision

Imaging the invisible
structure of the Earth

My long-term research goal is to advance subsurface imaging and reservoir characterisation using quantitative seismic inversion, joint geophysical modelling, and machine learning approaches — reducing uncertainty in subsurface interpretation for both energy security and sustainable resource management. I am particularly drawn to frontier environments: deepwater basins, under-explored inland basins, and the complex geological transitions that conventional methods struggle to resolve.

01
Quantitative Seismic Inversion

Developing simultaneous and model-based inversion workflows that extract rock physics properties directly from seismic amplitudes — bridging the gap between reflection data and reservoir description.

02
Joint Geophysical Modelling

Integrating seismic with gravity, magnetic, and electromagnetic datasets for better-constrained subsurface models — especially in frontier basins where well control is absent.

03
Frontier Basin Evaluation

Applying the full toolkit of exploration geophysics to Nigeria's under-explored inland and offshore basins — unlocking new hydrocarbon provinces and contributing to Africa's energy security.

04
Sustainable Geoscience

Extending geophysical methods toward the energy transition — carbon storage characterisation, geothermal exploration, and critical mineral discovery in Nigerian geological settings.

Scientific Methodology

How I Approach
Geophysical Problems

Every subsurface dataset is an incomplete record of geological reality. My approach is built on four interlocking principles that guide how I move from raw data to defensible interpretation.

"A geophysical interpretation is only as strong as your understanding of the acquisition system that produced the data, and the physical model that relates the data to the Earth."

— James Omeiza Bayode
Research Interests What I'm Reading Now →
1
Data Quality & Acquisition Geometry

Before any interpretation, I ask: what does this data actually record, and how well? Fold maps, offset-azimuth distributions, noise characterisation, and 4D repeatability metrics determine whether conclusions are trustworthy. Four years as an infield processor have made this instinct second nature.

2
Wave Physics & Inversion Frameworks

Seismic amplitudes encode rock and fluid properties — but only if you understand the convolution model, AVO gradients, and impedance contrasts well enough to invert them. I treat inversion not as a black box but as a constrained optimisation problem informed by rock physics and geology.

3
Geological Integration & Basin Context

Subsurface images only make sense within a stratigraphic and structural framework. I interpret seismic in the context of basin evolution, depositional systems, and tectonic history — connecting exploration geophysics to the geological story that created the prospect.

4
Uncertainty Quantification & Decision Support

Every interpretation carries uncertainty. I always ask: what would change my conclusion? Communicating uncertainty honestly — to a reservoir engineer, a drilling team, or a graduate supervisor — is as important as the interpretation itself.
