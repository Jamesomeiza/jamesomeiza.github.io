Research

Quantitative seismic methods, rock physics, and subsurface characterisation for energy exploration in West Africa and beyond.

Focus Areas

Research Interests

🔄
Seismic Inversion

Model-based, simultaneous, and stochastic seismic inversion approaches for deriving impedance, Vp/Vs, and density volumes for reservoir characterisation in deepwater clastic settings.

🪨
Rock Physics

Gassmann fluid substitution, effective medium modelling, and rock physics transforms linking petrophysical properties to elastic moduli — the foundation for seismic reservoir characterisation.

🔗
Joint Geophysical Inversion

Simultaneous inversion of seismic, gravity, and magnetic data to produce better-constrained subsurface models, particularly in areas of sparse well control.

🌍
Frontier Basin Evaluation

Regional geophysical assessment of Nigeria's inland basins — Anambra, Bida, Sokoto — for hydrocarbon prospectivity using potential field and seismic stratigraphy methods.

🕐
4D Seismic Reservoir Monitoring

Time-lapse seismic methods for monitoring reservoir fluid dynamics, pressure changes, and saturation movement during production — bridging field work and scientific analysis.

📐
Potential Field Methods

Application of gravity and magnetic data, including Euler deconvolution and spectral analysis, for structural mapping and basement depth estimation.

Undergraduate Thesis

Featured Research Project

3D Euler Deconvolution and Parameter Imaging of Magnetic Anomalous Bodies for Geotechnical Study of Part of OSUSTECH Main Campus

Institution Ondo State University of Science & Technology (OSUSTECH)
Year 2019
Type B.Sc. Undergraduate Thesis
Grade First Class

The Problem

Shallow subsurface features — buried foundations, dykes, metallic debris, or geotechnical anomalies — pose risks to civil engineering projects such as building construction, road development, and underground utility installation. Mapping these features accurately requires non-invasive geophysical methods that can resolve their depth, geometry, and nature without excavation.

Data & Study Area

High-resolution ground magnetic data were acquired across a portion of the OSUSTECH main campus using a proton precession magnetometer. A systematic grid pattern was used to ensure adequate spatial sampling. Diurnal variations were corrected using a stationary base station.

Methodology

3D Euler deconvolution was applied to the processed magnetic anomaly map. This method uses the spatial derivatives of the magnetic field and the Euler homogeneity relation to estimate the depth and position of anomalous sources without assumptions about source geometry. Structural index values were tested to identify the most appropriate source models. Parameter imaging of the total magnetic intensity (TMI) was also performed to enhance anomaly definition and geological interpretation.

Outcome

The study identified several shallow magnetic anomaly sources, estimated their depths and probable nature, and produced maps suitable for geotechnical risk assessment on the campus. Results demonstrated the viability of high-resolution ground magnetics for near-surface geotechnical investigation in similar geological settings.

Future Direction

Research Agenda

Looking ahead, James aims to pursue graduate research that deepens the scientific foundations of his industry practice.

MSc / PhD Focus 1
Seismic Inversion for Deepwater Reservoirs

Developing and testing simultaneous AVO inversion workflows on Niger Delta deepwater datasets to improve lithology and fluid discrimination beyond the wavelet-limited seismic bandwidth.

MSc / PhD Focus 2
Rock Physics of West African Turbidites

Building rock physics templates calibrated to Niger Delta deepwater clastic reservoirs to improve seismic reservoir characterisation and reduce subsurface uncertainty in field development decisions.

MSc / PhD Focus 3
Frontier Basin Potential Field Analysis

Regional gravity and magnetic study of Nigeria's inland sedimentary basins to evaluate petroleum prospectivity and sedimentary thickness — integrating potential field methods with available seismic data.
