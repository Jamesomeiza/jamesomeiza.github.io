Biography

About James

Geophysicist, exploration scientist, and mentor.

Origin Story

From Mathematics to the Subsurface

James Bayode initially set out to study mathematics — drawn by its rigour and its capacity to describe the physical world with precision. It was this same impulse that led him, ultimately, into geophysics: the discipline where mathematics meets the Earth, where equations become images of rock layers buried kilometres below the ocean floor.

He graduated from the Ondo State University of Science and Technology (OSUSTECH) with a first-class degree in Applied Geophysics, finishing at the top of his class. His undergraduate research involved the application of 3D Euler deconvolution to magnetic anomaly data — a study that laid the foundation for his interest in potential field methods and subsurface parameter estimation.

After a brief period in the Nigerian banking sector — which sharpened his analytical discipline and professional communication — James joined the Nigerian National Petroleum Company (NNPC) in 2020. There, he has been deployed on major offshore seismic campaigns across Nigeria's deep-water Niger Delta province.

"Geophysics is the art of inferring the invisible. Every dataset is a conversation with the Earth — and learning to listen is the life's work."

— James Bayode
Industry Experience

Marine Seismic Exploration

As an Infield Processor working in partnership with BGP International under NNPC Joint Ventures, James has accumulated hands-on experience across some of Nigeria's most technically demanding offshore seismic projects. His work spans both 3D full-fold acquisition surveys and 4D time-lapse monitoring programmes.

His role as infield processor is central to the quality and scientific integrity of the acquired data. He is responsible for real-time quality control (QC) of incoming seismic traces, noise attenuation, navigation processing, and near-field processing to ensure that the data acquired at sea meets the specifications required for subsequent onshore processing and final imaging.

Projects he has contributed to include the Bonga Field 3D and Bonga North 4D (Shell), Agbami 4D (Chevron), and the Egina 4D and Preowei 3D surveys (TotalEnergies) — all conducted in the ultra-deep waters of the Niger Delta, in water depths exceeding 1000 metres.

Research Vision

Bridging Industry and Academia

James's longer-term ambition is to pursue graduate research at the intersection of seismic inversion and rock physics. He is particularly interested in how quantitative seismic interpretation methods — model-based inversion, simultaneous inversion, and AVO analysis — can be used to characterise deepwater clastic reservoirs in the Niger Delta and other West African basins.

He is also motivated by the question of frontier basin evaluation: how can we use potential field data, regional seismic, and basin modelling to assess the hydrocarbon prospectivity of Nigeria's less-explored basins — the Anambra, Bida, and Sokoto basins — and contribute to energy security on the continent?

Beyond research, James is deeply committed to education and mentorship. He participates actively in the Nigerian Association of Petroleum Explorationists (NAPE) Young Professionals program, where he mentors undergraduate students in geoscience and helps bridge the gap between academic training and industry practice.
